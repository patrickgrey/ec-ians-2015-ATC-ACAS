Responsibilities for ATC and Pilots

A controller remains responsible for establishing and maintaining separations as long as no RA is being followed by an aircrew. When a pilot reports a manoeuvre, the controller shall not attempt to modify the aircraft trajectory, but should provide traffic information if deemed necessary. The controller shall resume responsibility for separation when the pilot reports 'Clear of conflict!' (see below).

It is important to emphasize that pilots are not to manoeuvre their aircraft solely on the basis of a TA, whose main objective is to prepare the crew for a possible RA. In the event of an RA being issued, a pilot remains in control of the aircraft, should visually monitor the airspace in which the intruder is indicated ¹, and has full authority to exercise his best judgement on the appropriate course of action. However, when deviating from the ATC clearance, the pilot should do so within the confines that will be indicated to him on his instrumentation by the system ². He should also return to the current clearance as soon as he is notified that the situation is resolved, i.e. the message 'Clear of conflict!' is annunciated in the cockpit, and the RA is no longer displayed on the instrumentation.

The pilot shall inform ATC of the RAs causing a deviation from the current clearance as soon as workload permits. After "Clear of Conflict" pilot shell return to the last clearance and notify ATC or seek alternative ATC instructions.
