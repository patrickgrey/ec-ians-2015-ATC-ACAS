Summary and Conclusion

Despite the general positive impact of TCAS, and it has now attained universal recognition in the world of aviation, it should be emphasised that it is a tool of 'last resort' designed to prevent mid-air collision when the primary means of separation has failed.

Furthermore, it is not a perfect system, and it cannot entirely eliminate all risks of collision. Additionally, as in any predictive system, it might itself induce a risk of collision.

Nevertheless, many of the failings in the system to date are due to human factors rather than the technology itself, and this is an area where training can help to improve the performance.

TCAS II does not provide:

  • warning of loss of standard separation,
  • prevention of close encounters, or
  • surveillance supporting lateral manoeuvres.
