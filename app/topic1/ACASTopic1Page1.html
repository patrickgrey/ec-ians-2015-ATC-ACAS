Module - ACAS
(Airborne Collision Avoidance System)

The Aims and Scope of This Module

The aim of this module is to provide air traffic controllers with essential theoretical knowledge related to the TCAS equipment, including some examples of its impact on operational procedures.

By the end of the module air traffic controllers will be able to react appropriately to TCAS RA's.

The module provides:

  • an overview of the ACAS concept,
  • a description of the provision of ATC,
  • a description of the responsibilities of both pilots and air traffic controllers during a TCAS RA encounter, and
  • an understanding of the TCAS II logic and its operation.

You can read the training objectives, as defined by 'ACAS/TCAS Training Syllabus for Air Traffic Controllers' on the next page.

Read these now and familiarise yourself with them. The objectives will also be clearly explained in the introduction to each topic.
