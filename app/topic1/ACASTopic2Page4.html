Evaluation of Operational Use

Evaluation of TCAS by EUROCONTROL has concluded that it is extremely effective and has significantly improved flight safety.

It allows aircrew to make visual aquisition of intruders based on TAs, and also provides highly reliable RAs. ACAS II improves safety by reducing the risk of mid-air collision by a factor of about 4. Danger occurs when pilots do not correctly respond to the RA.

Inevitably, some problems have been observed, and some of the most significant of these are listed below:

  • An RA can sometimes cause a pilot to deviate from his ATC clearance by far more than is necessary (see opposite).
  • Other problems of note relate to the misuse and/or misinterpretation of the data shown in TCAS displays by pilots.

Display in aircraft 'A' cockpit

towards the top of the screen.

The display in the cockpit, suggests that the aircraft above is flying towards aircraft A. In fact it is crossing at 90°. TCAS traffic displays show the relative positions of traffic not their headings.

Pilots can sometimes be confused by the TCAS traffic displays in the EFIS navigation display (EHSI) or the VS! in the cockpit.

Looking at the cockpit display and reading it as a radar picture, a pilot might be tempted to turn to the right if trying to avoid the other aircraft. This could be disastrous as it could take him even closer to an intruding aircraft. We shall return to this point in topics 2 and 3.
