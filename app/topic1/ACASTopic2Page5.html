Summary and Conclusion

Despite the general positive impact of TCAS, and it has now attained universal recognition in the world of aviation, it should be emphasised that it is a tool of 'last resort' designed to prevent mid-air collision when the primary means of separation has failed.

Furthermore, it is not a perfect system, and it cannot entirely eliminate all risks of collision. Additionally, as in any predictive system, it might itself induce a risk of collision.

Nevertheless, many of the failings in the system to date are due to human factors rather than the technology itself, and this is an area where training can help to improve the performance.

TCAS II does not provide:

  • warning of loss of standard separation,
  • prevention of close encounters, or
  • surveillance supporting lateral manoeuvres.

Preconditions to the effective utilisation of TCAS

The implementation of TCAS II brings a whole range of safety-related benefits when:

  • the ATC system provides a high quality service,
  • all airborne aircraft report their pressure-altitude via the transponder, and
  • pilots correctly follow the RA issued by the TCAS II system.

Understanding and awareness

Controllers, though aware of the improved flight safety due to the increating deployment of TCAS, also see some drawbacks. It is therefore essential that controllers have a good knowledge of the system's characteristics and of the procedures used by aircrew.

Controllers are also required to provide the same ATC service, especially with regard to traffic information or the maintenance of the relevant ATC separation, whether the aircraft are fitted with TCAS or not.

Vigilance

TAs and RAs are unplanned events, which call for fast and appropriate reactions from the crew, and therefore require specific training.

Nevertheless, even in aircraft with a TCAS II onboard, the crew must continue to maintain a visual lookout to avoid collisions, because some aircraft, either do not transmit their altitude via the transponder, or are invisible to the TCAS II system because they are not equipped with a transponder.

Proviso

However, one must be aware that ACAS is not a perfect system.

ACAS cannot preclude all collision risks. Consequently, it is essential that ATC procedures are designed to provide flight safety without any reliance upon the use of ACAS.
