Cockpit Presentation

The cockpit presentation provides limited information on adjacent traffic, (the traffic display), TAs and RAs, and aural annunciations.

The traffic information display system is designed to aid visual acquisition of an intruder. It indicates the relative horizontal and vertical position of other aircraft, in the vicinity, by measuring the replies from their transponders.

This information is integrated into cockpit displays, either the VSI in a 'classical' instrument system, or as part of the navigation display in the EFIS. The symbology used is the same.

RAs are often displayed on vertical speed indicators (VSI), such as the instantaneous vertical speed indicators or the vertical speed tape in the PFD. On some aircraft, RAs are displayed on the EADI (Electronic Attitude Display Indicator) by a pitch cue, which corresponds to the required vertical speed.

Loud speakers located in the cockpit alert the crew, by means of aural annunciation, of TCAS advisories (TAs and RAs).

The symbology used to represent traffic is very intuitive. Which symbol do you think corresponds to each category of traffic?

Click on each '?' then select the appropriate symbol.

Note, non-intruding aircraft, which are within 6 NM and 1200 ft from your own aircraft, are called 'proximate' traffic.

Targets are displayed by different symbols, according to their ACAS status:

  • a hollow blue or white diamond - for non intruding traffic,
  • a solid yellow circle - for intruders (which trigger a TA), and
  • a solid red square - for threats (which trigger an RA).

'Proximate' traffic are identified by a solid white or blue diamond. In the event of an advisory, this symbol indicates that the aircraft is not the intruder generating the advisory, illustrating that the closest traffic may not necessarily be the most threatening.

Each symbol is displayed on the screen according to its relative position to own aircraft. The display accuracy depends on the selected scale.

When the 10 NM scale is in use, the positional accuracy is approximately +/- 1 NM in range and +/- 10 degrees in bearing.

The 'own aircraft' symbol, representing the TCAS equipment host aircraft, is an arrowhead or aeroplane-like symbol coloured white or blue.

Vertical data is also shown with the relevant symbol if the intruder is reporting altitude. The relative altitude, in hundreds of feet, is displayed above the symbol if the intruder is own aircraft or it in the opposite case. In some aircraft, the flight level of the intruder can be displayed instead of its relative altitude. Finally, an arrow is shown when the target aircraft is climbing or descending at more than 500 ft/min.

The symbology, along with visual information relating to RAs, is integrated into the VSI (and EFIS displays).

An RA is a combination of the red and green arcs. The green arc indicates the required vertical rate and the red arc the vertical rates to avoid.

Note: two red arcs may flank the range of required vertical speeds if more than one threat is present.

The dotted circle around the own aircraft symbol in the VSI represents a radius of 2 nm. The display range can vary from 4 to 30 nm ahead of own aircraft.
